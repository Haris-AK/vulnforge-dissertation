# CM3203 formative feedback framework

Use this file for Cardiff Computer Science **CM3203 final report** formative feedback only.

This framework is for **developmental review**. It is not a marking scheme output and must not be converted into a mark prediction.

## Criteria

Use these exact headings:
- **Problem and Background**
- **Solution to the Problem**
- **Evaluation**
- **Communication and Project Management Skills**

## Criterion guidance

### Problem and Background

Assess:
- how clearly the problem is defined and situated
- clarity of aims, objectives, or research questions
- awareness and critical use of background and related work
- whether the chosen approach is well motivated by the problem framing

Typical improvement points:
- sharpen the exact problem statement
- state explicit aims, objectives, or research questions
- tighten the literature or background around the most relevant work
- explain more clearly how the background motivates the chosen approach

### Solution to the Problem

Assess:
- soundness of the approach and design
- quality, completeness, and appropriateness of the implementation
- justification of methods, tools, and design decisions
- whether the solution has appropriate depth for CM3203

Typical improvement points:
- separate requirements, design, and implementation more clearly
- justify key technical choices explicitly
- explain major components and how they address the problem
- make the student's own technical contribution clearer

### Evaluation

Assess:
- whether clear evaluation criteria are defined
- thoroughness and appropriateness of testing or experimentation
- critical appraisal of results, including limitations and failure cases
- whether the agreed deliverables appear substantially achieved

Typical improvement points:
- define explicit evaluation criteria tied to the project aims
- align tests or experiments with those criteria
- interpret results critically rather than just reporting success
- discuss limitations, failure cases, and unmet deliverables directly

### Communication and Project Management Skills

Assess:
- written communication, structure, and signposting
- use of figures, references, and academic style
- evidence of planning, control, and reflection
- quality of reflection, especially whether it analyses decisions and lessons learned rather than narrating activity

Typical improvement points:
- improve section introductions, signposting, and logical flow
- tighten presentation and referencing
- reflect on decisions, trade-offs, and lessons learned more analytically
- explain how the project was managed over time and what changed
